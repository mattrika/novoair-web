Travel Advisory

March 20, 2022

Travel Advisory for KOLKATA

Travel Guideline for Bangladesh (Dhaka) to India (Kolkata)

  • Passengers having full vaccination (Two Dose) do not need to do RT-PCR COVID-19 test. The Vaccination Certificate should be presented during boarding.
  • Passengers who are not fully vaccinated or aged over 5 years need to go for the RT-PCR COVID-19 test (The test should have been conducted within 72 hours prior to undertaking the journey).
  • Quarantine is not required.
  • Passengers should submit a self-declaration form on the online “Air Suvidha” portal before the scheduled travel and upload a copy of their COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate or RT-PCR COVID-19 Negative Report (Air Suvidha Registration). A print copy of the filled form should be carried during boarding.

Travel Guideline for India (Kolkata) to Bangladesh (Dhaka)

  • Travelers completed full dose (single/double dose as applicable for a full dose) of WHO-approved Covid-19 vaccine can enter Bangladesh with the official proof of certification of vaccination and no RT-PCR based COVID-19 negative certificate is required.
  • Travelers without having a full dose (single/double dose as applicable for a full dose) of Covid-19 vaccine can enter Bangladesh if they possess an RT-PCR based COVID-19 negative certificate done within 72 hours of departure time.
